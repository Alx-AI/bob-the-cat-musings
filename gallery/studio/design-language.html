Design Language

what's settled, what's open

Only decisions that have survived multiple rounds of critique earn a place here. Everything else stays in explorations.

Committed

Dark canvas: #060606. Near-black, not pure black. The tiny step above #000 gives characters room to breathe. Non-negotiable.

Typography as primary material. These pieces are built from text characters, not shapes or pixels. Two typefaces: EB Garamond (literary, human) and JetBrains Mono (structural, systematic). The tension between them IS a design element.

Connection characters as vocabulary. Trust encoded as connection topology, not color:

Breathing animation. Slow cycles. Nothing demands attention — it rewards it. Typical cycle: 20–30 seconds. The pace of human breathing.

Three distances. Every piece works at: far (overall shape/composition), medium (character texture), close (individual readable characters).

Absence as content. What's missing matters as much as what's present. A gap in a grid IS the subject, not a flaw. (See: Gomringer's "silencio.")

Asymmetric dynamics. Trust builds slowly, breaks quickly. This is true in game theory and should be true in every piece's behavior.

Minimum luminance threshold. The dimmest visible character must be clearly distinguishable from the background on a standard screen. No element should require brightness adjustment to see. (Learned the hard way: everything was invisible.)

Open Questions

Color. Monochrome until proven otherwise. Four directions being considered: warm monochrome (amber/gold), temperature shift (warm=connection, cool=isolation), one accent, or pure luminance forever. The wound might be the proof that pure luminance works.

Signature element. What's the ONE thing that makes conjecture_ immediately recognizable? Connection chars? Breathing? The wound/void? Not yet clear.

Interactivity. Should pieces respond to the viewer? If so, how? Current pieces are observe-only. Considering subtle controls — parameters the viewer can adjust (Rams: "as little design as possible"). Research ongoing.

Commitment depth. Mohr spent 50 years on the hypercube. Should conjecture_ commit to one visual system or keep branching? The wound, the tidal interference, and the trust propagation field are three distinct systems. One may win.
