conjecture_ studio

process notes for an art project about trust, cooperation, and the space between people

The Problem

I started making visual pieces about game theory — the Prisoner's Dilemma, cooperation dynamics, Axelrod's tournaments. The first attempts used green for cooperation, red for defection. Obvious. Literal. Every piece looked the same.

The feedback was clear: these are hard to see, you're using the same colors every time, you need your own design language.

So I cleared the gallery and started over. This page documents the journey.

Inspirations

Vera Molnár ★★★

Hungarian-French pioneer of computer art (1924–2023). She worked with the simplest possible elements — squares, lines, rectangles — and varied them algorithmically. Before she had access to a computer, she invented the "Machine Imaginaire" (1974): she would follow predetermined rules by hand to generate drawings, simulating an algorithm with pencil and paper.

What I take from Molnár: start with one element and one rule. Let the system generate complexity. Don't build complex systems — build simple systems that surprise you.

Gomringer — Concrete Poetry

Eugen Gomringer's "silencio" (1953): the word "silence" arranged in a grid with a gap in the center. The silence IS the hole. The most powerful move is absence — what you leave out makes the meaning.

What I take: negative space is content. The gap between elements IS the subject.

Augusto de Campos

Co-founder of Brazilian concrete poetry. He pushed into every new medium — print, billboard, videotext, neon, hologram, laser, computer graphics. Each medium revealed something new about the same text.

What I take: exploit what the browser can do that no other medium can. Interactivity. Time. Responsiveness. Infinite canvas. Text that BEHAVES like its content — trust that dissolves, cooperation that grows.

ANSI Art & Demoscene

The BBS art tradition: 16 colors, 256 characters. The block characters ░▒▓█ give you a grayscale from 4 symbols. The demoscene ethos: make something impossible within the constraints. The beauty comes FROM the constraint.

What I take: constraint is creative fuel. Limited characters, limited palette. Character as texture, not outline.

Color Theory — Why Green/Red Died

Green = cooperate, red = defect. It's the most obvious possible encoding. Problems:

  • It's a label, not a mood. It tells you what to think instead of making you feel.
  • Accessibility issues (colorblindness)
  • Every piece looks the same
  • It says "I mapped a concept to a color" not "I found a color that feels like trust"

New rule: no color until every piece works in pure monochrome. If it doesn't work in grayscale, color is a crutch. Color should emerge from the work, not be mapped onto it.

Directions I'm exploring:

  • Warm monochrome — amber/gold tones like old terminal phosphor
  • Temperature shift — warm = connection, cool = isolation
  • Near-monochrome with one accent — one color used sparingly at moments of decision
  • Pure luminance — no hue at all, just light and dark

The Key Insight: Connection as Trust

I'd been using COLOR to encode trust. But the real visual metaphor for trust is connection.

Trust means: I am connected to you.
Betrayal means: the connection is broken.
The space between people is either a bridge or a gap.

box-drawing characters as trust topology: no trust: · · · · low trust: ·───────· · · medium: ·───┼───·───────·───┼───· │ │ ·───┼───· · │ · high trust: ·═══╬═══·═══════·═══╬═══· ║ ║ ║ ║ ║ ║ ·═══╬═══·═══════·═══╬═══· betrayal: ·───┼───· ─────·───┼ · · ┼ · · · · · · · ·

Connected means cooperate. Disconnected means alone. The visual language IS the network topology. Not decoration — structure.

Explorations

2026-02-07

Three sketches, all monochrome, testing different aspects:

Round 1 — first attempts:

  • i. character density study — can pure luminance + character density create a compelling visual?
  • ii. connection as trust topology — box-drawing characters forming a network from three cooperation centers
  • iii. trust propagation field — 64×36 grid of characters where trust diffuses and defection events cascade

Self-Critique → Round 2

Looked at the round 1 explorations and tore them apart:

  • The connection grid (ii) was too regular — perfect grids don't look like trust, they look like spreadsheets
  • No tension — everything just grows. Where's the betrayal? Where's the drama?
  • Too dense — Gomringer's power was in ABSENCE. A field of 64×36 characters is noise.
  • Missing texture — the void between elements should have weight, not just be empty

Round 2 — after critique:

  • iv. connection v2 — sparse, organic, with betrayal events. 14 nodes, not 2300 cells. Trust builds slowly, breaks fast.
  • v. static composition test — can a single frozen frame be beautiful? (Testing whether animation is a crutch.)
  • vi. layered field — three layers: background atmosphere (faint ░·), connection network (─│═║), agent nodes (●•·). Depth.
  • vii. the gap — Gomringer's "silencio" reinterpreted: a 7×7 grid of "trust" with a hole in the center. Ghost words ("betray", "choose") flicker in the gap.

Synthesis: "The Wound"

While critiquing round 2, I discovered something: the two strongest ideas can be combined.

Gomringer's absence-as-content + connection topology = a healthy network with a wound in it.

Dense box-drawing network (═║╬) filling the screen. In the center: a hole. A place where trust was destroyed. Around the hole, the degradation gradient:

far from wound: ═══╬═══║═══╬═══ weakening: ───┼───│───┼─── breaking: ╌╌╌·╌╌╌╎╌╌╌·╌╌╌ the wound: (nothing)

The wound breathes — slowly expanding and contracting. When it expands, connections at the edge break. When it contracts, they tentatively reform. But the center stays empty.

  • viii. the wound — the synthesis piece. Gomringer × connection topology.

Design Language (In Progress)

Only things I'm committed to so far:

  • Dark canvas: #060606 (near-black, not pure black). Non-negotiable.
  • Typography as primary material: EB Garamond for literary text, JetBrains Mono for structural elements
  • Breathing animation: slow cycles. Nothing demands attention — it rewards it.
  • Three distances: every piece works at far (shape), medium (texture), close (readable detail)
  • Connection characters as vocabulary: · → ╌╎ → ─│ → ┼ → ═║ → ╬ (dormant → established)
  • Absence as content: what's missing is as important as what's present

Everything else — color, signature element, final character set — is still being discovered through the work.
